Know More About ABV

ABV aka alcohol by volume, is the standard measurement, used worldwide, to assess the strength of a particular beer. The ABV scale is simple in the fact that the higher the ABV, the more alcohol that beer contains. Lighter beers range from 4-4.5% ABV, with percentages getting higher with heavier styles of beer.

Know More about IBU

An IBU is a unit of measure of the bitterness of beer, which comes from hops. The more hoppy the beer, the higher the IBU. A beer's IBU is derived from a complicated formula and is typically way below 100, with blonde ales rating around 15 to 30 IBUs and stouts, like Guinness, going up to 60 IBUs.
