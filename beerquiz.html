Learn About ABV

ABV aka alcohol by volume, is the standard measurement, used worldwide, to assess the strength of a particular beer. The ABV scale is simple in the fact that the higher the ABV, the more alcohol that beer contains. For example, a beer with an abv of 5 is 5% alcohol.

  • Light beers range from 3% to just over 4% abv, and are extremely common. These are a bit more rare in craft beers, however.
  • A Medium beer will have roughly between 5% and 8% abv. These have a bit more bite, and often include IPAs and stouts. Many craft beers fall in this range.
  • A high abv beer is roughly above 8%, with 13% usually seen as the ceiling for abv in beer. However, some rare brews reach as high as 32% abv!
Learn About IBU

IBU aka international bitterness units, is a standardized way of determining the bitterness of a beer.This bitterness is defined by the concentration of bittering chemicals in beer, largely hops and malt.

  • A less bitter beer tends to have roughly less than 30 IBU, and is great for those that prefer sweet tastes.
  • A Medium beer will have an IBU of about 30-70. Most stouts will fall in here.
  • A High IBU beer tends to have above 70 IBU, and is a good fit for lovers of the depth a bitter taste. A close approximation would be high cacao chocolate. Most beers in this category will be IPAs
